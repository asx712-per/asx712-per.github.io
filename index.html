About

I'm a mechanical and systems engineer working at the intersection of physical simulation and machine learning. My core is thermal system design, FEA/CFD, and DIN/ISO-compliant prototype development.

My differentiator is what sits on top: I build ML surrogate models — including graph neural networks — that reproduce simulation results at a fraction of the compute, so design teams iterate in minutes instead of days.

Recent work spans a pyrolysis-reactor prototype validated against European pressure-vessel and piping standards, a predictive risk-factor framework for journal bearings, and a geothermal data-center cooling concept that cut energy use by 40%.

Native English speaker, based in Berlin, currently improving my German. Open to simulation, ML-for-engineering, and mechanical R&D roles across the EU and remote.
